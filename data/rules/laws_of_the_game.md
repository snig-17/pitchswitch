# Laws of the Game — plain-English rules for casual fans

Concise explanations of common in-match events, grounded in the IFAB/FIFA Laws
of the Game. Used by "Coach" (Granite + Docling) to explain what just happened.

## Penalty (Law 14 — The Penalty Kick)

A penalty is awarded when a defender commits a direct-free-kick foul or a
deliberate handball inside their own penalty area. The attacking team gets one
shot from the penalty mark, twelve yards out, with only the goalkeeper to beat.

## Foul (Law 12 — Fouls and Misconduct)

A foul is given when a player challenges an opponent carelessly, recklessly, or
with excessive force — a trip, push, hold, kick, or handball. The opposing team
restarts play with a free kick from where the offence happened.

## Card (Law 12 — Fouls and Misconduct)

A yellow card is a formal caution for offences such as a reckless foul, dissent,
or time-wasting. A red card sends the player off for a serious offence (violent
play, denying a clear goal-scoring opportunity, or a second yellow) and forces
their team to play the rest of the match a player short.

## Corner (Law 17 — The Corner Kick)

A corner kick is awarded when the ball wholly crosses the goal line, last touched
by a defender, without a goal being scored. The attacking team restarts from the
corner arc nearest where it went out and usually delivers it into the box.

## Goal (Law 10 — Determining the Outcome of a Match)

A goal is scored when the whole of the ball crosses the goal line between the
posts and under the crossbar. It stands only if no offence (offside, handball, or
foul) was committed by the attacking team in the build-up.
